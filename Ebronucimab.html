Ebronucimab (AK102)
Akeso Inc., China
PCSK9 Monoclonal Antibody
NMPA Approved 2024

📋 Detailed Information

Mechanism of Action

Ebronucimab is a fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody that specifically binds to PCSK9 in circulation. By inhibiting PCSK9's ability to bind to and promote degradation of LDL receptors on hepatocytes, it prevents lysosomal degradation of LDL-R. This leads to:

  • Increased LDL-R expression on liver surface
  • Enhanced hepatic uptake and clearance of LDL-C
  • Significant reductions in circulating LDL-C levels (50-70%)
  • Rapid onset within 1-2 weeks
  • High specificity with minimal impact on triglycerides or HDL-C

Target Population

Adults with primary hypercholesterolemia (including heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia) and mixed dyslipidemia who are:

  • On maximally tolerated statin therapy
  • Statin-intolerant patients
  • Requiring additional LDL-C reduction beyond current therapy

Neurocognitive Safety

No signals in trials despite 20-30% achieving very low LDL (<25 mg/dL). Memory impairment and confusion <1%, similar to placebo. Consistent with broader PCSK9 class data showing no association with cognitive decline even at LDL <15 mg/dL over 5+ years.

🔮 Pipeline & Sustainability

Current Status

  • No new formulations or indications in pipeline
  • Focus on post-approval real-world studies (especially HeFH)
  • No cardiovascular outcomes trials planned

Future Outlook

  • China Market: Strong position as local champion, government support
  • Global Potential: Limited without Western partner; potential EMA/FDA entry 2027-2028 if partnered
  • Competitive Threats: Biosimilars of first-gen mAbs and oral PCSK9 inhibitors (e.g., Merck's) by 2030
  • Sustainability: May become niche option for refractory cases without outcomes data
⚡ Key Differentiator: Q6W dosing offers improved convenience (8-9 injections/year) but still more frequent than inclisiran's twice-yearly schedule. Success depends on aggressive pricing and NRDL inclusion.
