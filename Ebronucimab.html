Key Information

Mechanism of Action

Fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody that binds circulating PCSK9, preventing LDL receptor degradation. This leads to:

  • Increased hepatic LDL receptor expression
  • Enhanced LDL-C clearance from circulation
  • 50-70% LDL-C reduction within 1-2 weeks
  • Minimal impact on triglycerides or HDL-C

Target Population

Adults with primary hypercholesterolemia (including HeFH) and mixed dyslipidemia who:

  • Are on maximally tolerated statin therapy
  • Are statin-intolerant
  • Require additional LDL-C reduction beyond current therapy

Neurocognitive Safety

No signals in trials despite 20-30% achieving very low LDL (<25 mg/dL). Memory impairment and confusion <1%, similar to placebo. Consistent with PCSK9 class data showing no cognitive decline even at LDL <15 mg/dL over 5+ years.

Immunogenicity

As a fully human monoclonal antibody, immunogenic potential is low. Anti-drug antibodies detected in 1-3% of patients with no impact on efficacy or safety. No neutralizing antibodies reported.

Special Populations

  • Elderly: No dose adjustment needed, similar safety profile
  • Renal impairment: No dose adjustment (mAbs not renally cleared)
  • Hepatic impairment: Limited data, use with caution
  • Pregnancy: Category B equivalent (animal studies negative)

Pricing Strategy

Expected ¥8,000-12,000/year (approximately $1,100-1,650 USD), positioning 30-50% below imported PCSK9 mAbs like Repatha. Final pricing pending NRDL negotiations.

Reimbursement Outlook

Not yet on National Reimbursement Drug List (NRDL). Inclusion in 2025-2026 cycles could provide 70-80% coverage, dramatically improving access. Government CV disease initiatives favor domestic products.

Current reimbursement coverage: ~10%

Competitive Advantages

  • Q6W dosing offers improved convenience (8-9 injections/year)
  • Domestic production avoids import costs and supply chain issues
  • Government "Healthy China 2030" initiative supports local innovation
  • First domestic PCSK9 mAb in large Chinese market

Market Challenges

  • Inclisiran entering China with 2x/year dosing advantage
  • Oral PCSK9 inhibitors coming by 2026-2027
  • No CV outcomes data vs proven benefit for Repatha/Praluent
  • Limited global expansion potential without partner
