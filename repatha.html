PCSK9 Inhibition via Monoclonal Antibody

Repatha is a fully human monoclonal antibody that specifically binds to proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 (PCSK9) in the bloodstream. By inhibiting PCSK9's interaction with LDL receptors (LDL-R) on hepatocytes, it prevents receptor degradation.

Key Mechanisms:

  • â€¢ Increased LDL-R recycling on liver surface
  • â€¢ Enhanced clearance of LDL cholesterol from blood
  • â€¢ Substantial reductions in circulating LDL-C levels (50-70%)
  • â€¢ Rapid onset of effect (peak reduction within 1-2 weeks)

Key Differentiators: Unlike statins (which upregulate LDL-R expression) or inclisiran (which silences PCSK9 gene via siRNA), evolocumab acts post-translationally on circulating PCSK9, providing immediate effect without genetic modification.
