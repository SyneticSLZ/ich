Repatha® (evolocumab)
PCSK9 Monoclonal Antibody
Amgen • FDA/EMA Approved 2015

Mechanism of Action

PCSK9 Inhibition via Monoclonal Antibody
Repatha is a fully human monoclonal antibody that specifically binds to proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 (PCSK9) in the bloodstream. By inhibiting PCSK9's interaction with LDL receptors (LDL-R) on hepatocytes, it prevents receptor degradation, leading to:
  • Increased LDL-R recycling on liver surface
  • Enhanced clearance of LDL cholesterol from blood
  • Substantial reductions in circulating LDL-C levels (50-70%)
  • Rapid onset of effect (peak reduction within 1-2 weeks)
Key Differentiators: Unlike statins (which upregulate LDL-R expression) or inclisiran (which silences PCSK9 gene via siRNA), evolocumab acts post-translationally on circulating PCSK9, providing immediate effect without genetic modification.

Patient Populations

Approved Indications
Adults:
  • Heterozygous Familial Hypercholesterolemia (HeFH)
  • Homozygous Familial Hypercholesterolemia (HoFH)
  • Clinical ASCVD requiring additional LDL-C lowering
  • CV risk reduction (MI, stroke, coronary revascularization)

  • HeFH or HoFH patients
Note: No dose adjustments required for renal/hepatic impairment, age, or weight

FDA Approval Timeline

August 27, 2015
Initial FDA Approval
Approved for HeFH, clinical ASCVD, and HoFH in adults/adolescents 13+. Second PCSK9 inhibitor approved in US.
Adjunct to diet and maximally tolerated statin therapy. No CRLs or safety delays during review.
July 11, 2016
Pushtronex System Approval
First single monthly injection device for 420 mg dosing (on-body infusor).
Automated delivery system, though later discontinued in some markets due to patient experience issues.
December 1, 2017
CV Outcomes Indication
First PCSK9 inhibitor with outcomes-based indication for MI, stroke, and revascularization reduction.
Based on FOURIER trial data showing 15% MACE reduction in established CVD patients.
February 2021
Pediatric Expansion
Age limit lowered from 13+ to 10+ years for HeFH/HoFH patients.
Based on HAUSER-RCT pediatric trial showing safety and efficacy in younger patients.
2024
Label Updates
Pregnancy/lactation data added from post-marketing reports.
Insufficient evidence of fetal harm but discontinuation advised during pregnancy. No new safety signals.

FOURIER Trial - Detailed Outcomes

Primary & Secondary Endpoints
Primary Composite (MACE-5):
  • CV death, MI, stroke, UA hospitalization, coronary revascularization
  • HR 0.85 (95% CI: 0.79-0.92)
  • 20% relative risk reduction, p<0.001
Key Secondary (MACE-3):
  • CV death, MI, stroke
  • HR 0.80 (95% CI: 0.73-0.88)
  • 20% relative risk reduction
Note on Mortality: No significant reduction in CV death (HR 1.05) or all-cause mortality (HR 1.04), likely due to short median follow-up of 2.2 years. Longer follow-up in FOURIER-OLE showed trend toward mortality benefit.

Neurocognitive Safety

EBBINGHAUS Study Results
Primary Finding: No impact on cognitive function despite very low LDL-C levels

Cognitive Domains Tested:
  • Executive function
  • Working memory
  • Memory function
  • Psychomotor speed
Results:
  • Neurocognitive AEs: 1.6% vs 1.5% placebo
  • No correlation with LDL level
  • No difference in any cognitive domain
  • 8+ year follow-up confirms safety
2024 Update: Study of 3,000+ patients with very low LDL (<20 mg/dL) showed no cognitive association over 5+ years. Meta-analyses confirm no neurocognitive risk.

Market Access Challenges

Prior Authorization Requirements:
  • ~80% initial denial rate in US
  • Must prove statin failure/intolerance
  • LDL threshold requirements (>100-130 mg/dL)
  • Documentation of CV risk factors
  • Step therapy through other lipid agents
Payer Restrictions:
  • Express Scripts/UnitedHealth: Strict step therapy protocols
  • VA/CMS: Negotiated discounts but limited to severe cases
  • NICE/HTA: Only for LDL >3.5-5 mmol/L in high-risk (ICER >£30,000/QALY)
