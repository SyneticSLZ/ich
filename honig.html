Mechanism of Action
AZD0780 is an investigational oral small-molecule inhibitor of PCSK9. It binds with high affinity to the C-terminal domain of PCSK9, stabilizing the PCSK9-LDL receptor (LDL-R) complex and preventing PCSK9-mediated lysosomal degradation of LDL-R on hepatocytes. This unique mechanism allows for:
  • Increased recycling and surface expression of LDL-R
  • Enhanced hepatic uptake and clearance of LDL-C
  • Substantial reductions in serum LDL-C levels (~50%)
  • >90% reduction in free PCSK9 levels
Unlike macrocyclic peptides (e.g., MK-0616), AZD0780 is a traditional small molecule, potentially offering advantages in manufacturing scalability and tissue distribution. Its extracellular action minimizes off-target intracellular effects seen with some lipid therapies.

Key Information

What is VERVE-101/102?

An investigational gene-editing therapy using CRISPR-derived base-editing technology to permanently reduce cholesterol. It makes a single-letter change in the DNA of liver cells to turn off PCSK9 production forever.

  • Uses adenine base editor (ABE) - no double-strand DNA breaks
  • Delivered via lipid nanoparticle (LNP) to liver
  • Single IV infusion intended to last a lifetime
  • Mimics natural PCSK9 mutations that protect against heart disease

Current Status

  • VERVE-101: Paused April 2024 due to safety concerns (liver enzyme elevation, low platelets)
  • VERVE-102: Improved version with better LNP formulation, IND cleared March 2025
  • Heart-2 Trial: Starting H2 2025 with VERVE-102
  • Lilly Acquisition: Completed July 25, 2025 for ~$1 billion [Source]

Key Information

Mechanism of Action

Fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody that binds circulating PCSK9, preventing LDL receptor degradation. This leads to:

  • Increased hepatic LDL receptor expression
  • Enhanced LDL-C clearance from circulation
  • 50-70% LDL-C reduction within 1-2 weeks
  • Minimal impact on triglycerides or HDL-C

Target Population

Adults with primary hypercholesterolemia (including HeFH) and mixed dyslipidemia who:

  • Are on maximally tolerated statin therapy
  • Are statin-intolerant
  • Require additional LDL-C reduction beyond current therapy
