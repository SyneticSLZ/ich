ðŸŽ¯ Executive Summary

Current Market Reality

  • Statins remain foundation - 90% of patients start here (generic, ~$10/month)
  • PCSK9 antibodies proven but underused - High cost ($5,800/year) limits access
  • Inclisiran's unique position - Only twice-yearly therapy, NHS deal shows potential
  • Step therapy barriers - Must fail 2-3 drugs before accessing advanced therapies

What's Coming Next

  • Oral PCSK9 revolution (2025-26) - Daily pills matching injectable efficacy
  • Price competition intensifying - Biosimilars coming 2028, generics 2033+
  • Gene editing on horizon - One-time cure potential by 2030
  • Combination strategies emerging - Triple/quadruple therapy for ultra-low LDL

ðŸ’¡ Key Insight: The market is transitioning from "injectable vs oral" to "convenience vs compliance vs cost". Inclisiran's twice-yearly dosing offers guaranteed compliance, oral PCSK9i offers convenience, and biosimilars will offer cost savings. Success will depend on proving real-world outcomes and securing favorable payer agreements.

What is LDL-C?
Low-Density Lipoprotein Cholesterol
"Bad cholesterol" that builds up in arteries
Goal: <70 mg/dL (high-risk)
Goal: <55 mg/dL (very high-risk)
What is MACE?
Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events
Combined measure of heart attacks, strokes, CV death, and stents/bypass
What is PCSK9?
Proprotein Convertase Subtilisin/Kexin 9
Protein that destroys LDL receptors. Blocking = more LDL removal

Comparing approval pathways and regulatory divergence in cholesterol therapies

ðŸŽ¯ Regulatory Divergence Overview

  • EMA leads by 12 months average - Inclisiran approved Dec 2020 vs Dec 2021 FDA

  • EMA broader pediatric access - Evinacumab from 6 months vs FDA 5 years

Regulatory Implications

  • COVID impact on FDA - Facility inspections delayed multiple approvals
  • Harmonized CV standards - MACE endpoint requirements similar
  • Gene editing caution - Both agencies requiring extensive safety data
  • Label expansion timing - Post-approval evidence similarly evaluated

ðŸ’¡ Key Insight: While approval timing differs, both agencies maintain similar safety and efficacy standards. EMA's earlier approvals often reflect different risk tolerance and inspection priorities rather than lower standards. FDA's manufacturing focus has caused delays but not clinical rejections.

Compliance History & Regulatory Analysis

Minimal regulatory friction, mostly CMC-related issues

ðŸŽ¯ Compliance Overview

  • No OPDP enforcement - Zero warning letters for PCSK9 class to date
  • COVID exposed vulnerabilities - Third-party facility access became critical
  • Novel modalities scrutinized - Gene editing faced 11-month hold

Regulatory Success Factors

  • Clean safety profiles - No clinical safety-driven CRLs
  • Smooth label expansions - CV outcomes added without friction
  • 8+ years post-market success - Repatha/Praluent proven track records
  • Established tech advantage - mAbs and small molecules face less scrutiny

ðŸ’¡ Key Insight: The cholesterol therapy space shows remarkably clean compliance history with regulatory issues limited to manufacturing/CMC rather than safety or efficacy. Third-party manufacturing readiness and novel modality safety data are the primary regulatory risk factors.

Market Access & Payer Considerations

Typical Step Therapy Sequence:

  1. 1 Maximally tolerated statin (generic, ~$5-10/month)
  2. 2 Add ezetimibe (generic, ~$10-20/month)
  3. 3 Consider bempedoic acid (if statin intolerant, ~$400/month)
  4. 4 PCSK9 inhibitor consideration (if LDL still >70-100 mg/dL)
  5. 5 Choice between: Inclisiran (2x/year), mAbs (monthly/biweekly), or oral PCSK9i (when available)
