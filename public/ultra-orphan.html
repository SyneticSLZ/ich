Ultra‑Orphan CMC Precedents — Evidence Pack

Curated, citable precedents demonstrating that FDA has approved ultra‑orphan therapies while allowing Chemistry, Manufacturing & Controls (CMC) work to continue post‑approval (via PMRs/PMCs, interim methods, or concurrent validation). Designed to support arguments of potential inconsistent application of guidance in contexts like Barth syndrome.

Thesis

In ultra‑orphan settings, FDA has repeatedly approved products while finishing CMC under post‑marketing requirements/commitments (PMRs/PMCs), interim methods, or concurrent validation, when core controls were adequate at action.

Actual CMC/manufacturing deficiencies — approved with PMR/PMC or concurrent validation

These are not just "acceptable" findings — FDA explicitly called out incomplete/deficient CMC work at action and still approved, assigning the work to PMRs/PMCs or permitting concurrent validation due to rarity. Each entry includes contextual quotation and a jump link to the relevant page within the official FDA PDF.

MEPSEVII (vestronidase alfa‑vjbk) — 2017 · BLA 761047

Ultragenyx · Enzyme replacement (MPS VII)

Exact FDA language

“The CMC team found the existing manufacturing process acceptable. Additional validation will be required post approval. This concurrent validation approach to support approval is being used due to the rarity of the disease population.”
View in document (Medical Review, p. 28)

Why it matters

Explicit recognition of incomplete process validation at approval, justified by ultra‑rare context. Clean precedent for approval‑with‑validation‑continuing.

ONPATTRO (patisiran) — 2018 · NDA 210922

Alnylam · siRNA LNP

Exact FDA language

“It is noted that the review team and the applicant have identified concerns related to the robustness of the siRNA in vitro release method. The current method is considered acceptable on an interim basis. The Applicant has agreed to develop a new validated and robust in vitro drug release method as part of Post Marketing Commitment within 12 months…”
View in document (Chemistry Review, p. 12)

Why it matters

FDA approved while acknowledging a non‑robust, non‑validated release method, forcing a post‑approval method replacement (PMC). Clear allowance despite a method deficiency.

ZYNTEGLO (betibeglogene autotemcel) — 2022 · BLA 125717

bluebird bio · Autologous LVV gene therapy

Exact FDA language

“The following issues were identified but could not be resolved during the review cycle. These issues will be resolved through PMR/PMCs by March 30, 2024. Several unresolved issues were present for the final container…”
View in document (SBRA, p. 6)

Why it matters

Approval with a heavy CMC tail (including E&L on final container) is a direct precedent for handling unresolved CMC issues via PMR/PMCs rather than a CRL.

LUXTURNA (voretigene neparvovec) — 2017 · BLA 125610

Spark · AAV2 ocular gene therapy

Exact FDA language

“The review team recommends regular approval… with the Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Control (CMC) Postmarketing Commitments listed in Section 11.c…”
View in document (SBRA, p. 2)
Section 11.c details include shipping validation protocol/report, assay verification studies, and visual inspection sampling plan revisions. Jump to Section 11.c (p. 18)

Why it matters

Shows approval with specific, unfinished CMC tasks assigned post‑approval, including worst‑case shipping validation and E&L.

OXERVATE (cenegermin‑bkbj) — 2018 · BLA 761094

Dompé · Ophthalmic biologic

Exact FDA language

“To perform real time shipping validation studies to support the stability of OXERVATE (cenegermin‑bkbj)… [and] complete data and the risk evaluation for potential impact of leachables on product safety and quality will be provided in the final study report.”
View in document (Approval Letter, p. 6)

Why it matters

FDA approved while requiring real‑time shipping validation under worst‑case conditions and a full E&L assessment as post‑approval commitments.

Exhibit A — Heaviest hitters (ranked)

Each item includes the exact FDA quote (with page marker if visible), a direct link, and a short argument for why it supports approval‑with‑CMC‑work versus a CMC CRL.

MEPSEVII (vestronidase alfa‑vjbk) — 2017 · BLA 761047

Ultragenyx · Enzyme replacement · Ultra‑rare MPS VII

“This concurrent validation approach to support approval is being used due to the rarity of the disease population.”

Source: FDA Medical Review (BLA 761047). PDF

Why it’s gold: FDA explicitly accepts concurrent validation due to rarity, with additional validation post‑approval.

Use it to argue: If rarity justified concurrent validation here, issuing a CMC CRL for Barth—when issues are addressable via commitments—suggests inconsistent application.

ONPATTRO (patisiran) — 2018 · NDA 210922

Alnylam · siRNA LNP

“The current [release] method is considered acceptable on an interim basis… The Applicant has agreed to develop a new validated and robust in vitro drug release method as part of Post Marketing Commitment within 12 months from the NDA’s action date.”

Source: FDA Chemistry Review (NDA 210922). PDF

Why it’s gold: FDA allowed a temporary method with a required post‑approval replacement (PMC) and acknowledged inspectional ties.

Use it to argue: Squarely supports “approve now / finish CMC later,” not CRL.

ZYNTEGLO (betibeglogene autotemcel) — 2022 · BLA 125717

bluebird bio · Autologous LVV gene therapy

“Recommended Action: The Review Committee recommends approval…” (SBRA). “The following issues were identified but could not be resolved during the review cycle… resolved through PMR/PMCs… additional testing… and leachable testing on the final container as a PMR.”

Source: FDA SBRA (Aug 17, 2022). PDF

Why it’s strong: Approval accompanied by a safety PMR and multiple CMC PMCs (including E&L), i.e., a heavy CMC tail.

Use it: Shows FDA’s comfort approving complex products while pushing meaningful CMC to PMR/PMC management.

LUXTURNA (voretigene neparvovec) — 2017 · BLA 125610

Spark · AAV2 ocular gene therapy

“The review team recommends regular approval… with the Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Control (CMC) Postmarketing Commitments listed in Section 11.c… (1) provide the shipping validation study protocol… [and] final study report… (2) complete verification studies for assays… (6) revise procedures for visual inspection to incorporate statistically sound sampling plans…”

Source: FDA SBRA (Dec 18, 2017). PDF

Why it’s strong: Explicit CMC PMCs including shipping validation (worst‑case), sampling plans, and assay work — all post‑approval.

OXERVATE (cenegermin‑bkbj) — 2018 · BLA 761094

Dompé · Ophthalmic biologic

PMCs include: “To perform real‑time shipping validation… using worst‑case shipping conditions…” and “Complete extractables/leachables assessment…”

Sources: FDA Approval Letter (Aug 22, 2018) & Summary (Aug 31, 2018). Approval Letter PDF · Summary PDF

Why it’s strong: FDA allowed key control‑strategy elements to be finished post‑approval.

How to frame the argument

  1. Lead with MEPSEVII — explicit concurrent validation due to rarity.
  2. Follow with ONPATTRO — interim method acceptable + PMC for a new validated method (even with inspectional concerns).
  3. Stack ZYNTEGLO / LUXTURNA / OXERVATE — multiple modalities where CMC was finished post‑approval.
  4. Close with the pattern set — ZOLGENSMA, HEMGENIX, ROCTAVIAN, ELEVIDYS, CRYSVITA.

Conclusion: If Stealth’s Barth program issues are of the same class — validation, method refinement, E&L, shipping validation — a CMC CRL looks like a harsher‑than‑usual application relative to ultra‑orphan precedents.
