1.1 Trial Design & Endpoints ▼

VERITAC-2 Phase 3 Design Overview

VERITAC-2 (NCT05654623) is a global randomized Phase 3 study enrolling 624 patients across 26 countries, comparing once-daily oral vepdegestrant (200mg) versus intramuscular fulvestrant (500mg) in ER+/HER2− advanced breast cancer after progression on a CDK4/6 inhibitor plus endocrine therapy.

ITT Population Results: In the overall intent-to-treat population, vepdegestrant did not reach statistical significance - median PFS 3.7 vs 3.6 months (HR 0.83, 95% CI 0.68-1.02, p=0.07). This reflects that vepdegestrant's benefit is concentrated in tumors harboring ESR1 mutations, likely due to those mutations driving resistance to prior aromatase inhibitors.

Objective Response Rate: In ESR1-mutant patients, ORR was 18.6% with vepdegestrant versus 4.0% with fulvestrant (OR 5.45, p=0.001). Responses were durable with median duration not reached at data cutoff.

Overall Survival Data: OS data remain immature with less than 25% of events recorded. However, the observed efficacy on PFS and tumor response is compelling for this refractory population and parallels elacestrant's Phase 3 results (HR ~0.55, median PFS 3.8 vs 1.9 months in ESR1-mut patients).

1.3 Phase 2 Data & Transition to Phase 3 ▼

The VERITAC Phase 2 expansion provided strong rationale for Phase 3 advancement. In heavily pretreated ER+ breast cancer patients (median 3-4 prior lines), vepdegestrant monotherapy achieved clinical benefit rate ~47% in ESR1-mutant tumors and median PFS ~5.5 months in that subgroup.

This signal is remarkably consistent with the Phase 3 ESR1-mutant PFS (5.0 months), indicating a reproducible effect size. Some patients in Phase 2 remained on drug ≥48 weeks. The Phase 2 also demonstrated substantial ER degradation and tumor marker reduction.

Interim Analyses: There was no early termination for futility or overwhelming efficacy. The readout was at the planned final PFS analysis (58% of PFS events in vepdegestrant arm), suggesting straightforward trial conduct. An earlier topline announcement in March 2025 noted the positive ESR1-mutant result, with data then presented at ASCO 2025 as a late-breaker and published in NEJM.

2. Scientific & Intellectual Property Diligence
2.1 Mechanism of Action & Scientific Rationale ▼

PROTAC Technology Innovation

Vepdegestrant (ARV-471) is a first-in-class oral PROTAC (Proteolysis-Targeting Chimera) targeting the estrogen receptor. It consists of an ER-binding ligand linked to an E3 ubiquitin ligase recruiter. By binding both, it tags the ER for destruction via the proteasome.

This mechanism leads to near-complete ERα protein degradation in preclinical models. Vepdegestrant degrades both wild-type ER and mutant ER (including ESR1 ligand-binding domain mutations that confer resistance to aromatase inhibitors and fulvestrant).

Scientific Validation: ER is a well-known oncogenic driver in ~70% of breast cancers. Estrogen deprivation or receptor blockade improves survival in this disease. However, existing endocrine therapies have limitations - tumors acquire resistance often via ESR1 mutations that keep ER signaling active.

Advantages over Fulvestrant: Fulvestrant, a SERD, can degrade ER but has incomplete and variable degradation due to suboptimal PK and need for injections. Vepdegestrant's PROTAC approach achieves more potent and comprehensive ER knockdown inside tumor cells. Preclinical comparisons showed vepdegestrant causing greater tumor shrinkage than fulvestrant in ER-driven xenograft models, with additive efficacy when combined with CDK4/6 inhibitors.

Historic Achievement: Vepdegestrant is the first PROTAC ever to demonstrate improved outcomes in a Phase 3 trial, essentially validating targeted protein degradation in oncology. This novelty differentiates vepdegestrant from "me-too" SERDs.

Key Scientific Differentiators: Biochemically, PROTACs can achieve >90% degradation of target protein in cells, whereas traditional SERDs often plateau at lower levels. Clinically, vepdegestrant's 5.0-month median PFS in ESR1-mut patients versus 2.1 with fulvestrant was a 2.9-month benefit - modestly superior to elacestrant's 1.9-month benefit.

The success of vepdegestrant where others failed (Sanofi's amcenestrant, Lilly's imlunestrant both had Phase 3 failures) suggests achieving sufficient ER antagonism or degradation is challenging. Vepdegestrant's success implies a scientific edge through the PROTAC mechanism.

2.3 Combination Biology & Future Development ▼

Combination Studies

CDK4/6 Inhibitor Combination: Phase 1b combination with palbociclib showed good efficacy (median PFS 13.9 months, CBR ~63%) but revealed pharmacokinetic interaction requiring dose adjustment. Vepdegestrant increased palbociclib exposure, possibly via metabolic interaction (CYP3A4).

mTOR Inhibitor Combination: Phase 1b TACTIVE-E trial combining vepdegestrant with everolimus was initiated to overcome PI3K/AKT-mediated resistance. This could open use in ESR1-mutated, PI3K-activated tumors.

Neoadjuvant Setting: Vepdegestrant is being tested in early breast cancer to determine if profound ER degradation can shrink tumors pre-surgery. This could position it for adjuvant or earlier-line usage eventually.

Drug-Drug Interactions

P-gp Substrate Interaction: Coadministration doubled dabigatran exposure and caused minor (~11%) rosuvastatin exposure increase in healthy volunteers. Vepdegestrant can clinically affect P-gp substrates, requiring label warnings and management strategies.
