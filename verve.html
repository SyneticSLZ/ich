Key Information

What is VERVE-101/102?

An investigational gene-editing therapy using CRISPR-derived base-editing technology to permanently reduce cholesterol. It makes a single-letter change in the DNA of liver cells to turn off PCSK9 production forever.

  • Uses adenine base editor (ABE) - no double-strand DNA breaks
  • Delivered via lipid nanoparticle (LNP) to liver
  • Single IV infusion intended to last a lifetime
  • Mimics natural PCSK9 mutations that protect against heart disease

Current Status

  • VERVE-101: Paused April 2024 due to safety concerns (liver enzyme elevation, low platelets)
  • VERVE-102: Improved version with better LNP formulation, IND cleared March 2025
  • Heart-2 Trial: Starting H2 2025 with VERVE-102
  • Lilly Acquisition: Completed July 25, 2025 for ~$1 billion [Source]

Technical Specifications

  • Editor Type: Adenine Base Editor (ABE) - Cas9 nickase + TadA deaminase
  • Target: PCSK9 gene exon 1 - introduces premature stop codon
  • Delivery: Liver-targeted LNP (similar to COVID vaccines but optimized)
  • Editing Efficiency: 50-70% of hepatocytes at therapeutic doses
  • Off-target Rate: <0.1% genome-wide (WGS validated)
  • Germline Risk: Minimal - no gonadal LNP distribution shown [Source]

Brain Safety & Low Cholesterol

No Cognitive Risk Expected

PCSK9 inhibition doesn't cross blood-brain barrier. Brain cholesterol is synthesized independently from plasma LDL.

  • Genetic PCSK9 knockouts show no cognitive deficits
  • Meta-analyses of PCSK9 inhibitors (LDL <20 mg/dL) show no dementia risk over 5+ years
  • All trials monitor neurocognition - no signals reported
  • EBBINGHAUS study (evolocumab) confirmed cognitive safety

Precedents & Competition

Casgevy (Vertex/CRISPR)
FDA/EMA 2023

First CRISPR therapy approved for sickle cell. Price: $2.2M. Ex vivo editing (not in vivo like VERVE). Shows regulatory pathway possible, high price accepted for genetic diseases.

NTLA-2001 (Intellia)
Phase 3

In vivo CRISPR for ATTR amyloidosis. 87% TTR reduction, no major safety issues. Most similar precedent - shows in vivo editing feasible.

Zolgensma (Novartis)
FDA/EMA 2019

One-time AAV gene therapy for SMA. Price: $2.1M. Payer acceptance of high-cost cures, but 20-30% abandonment rate.
