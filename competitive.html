Comprehensive Phase 1/1a Analysis (NCT04557449)

Design Rigor: Open-label, multicenter dose-escalation study with 3+3 design progressing to expansion cohorts. No placebo or concurrent control arm - treatment effect inferred relative to endocrine therapy alone or historical benchmarks.

DLT Window & RP2D Determination: Dose-limiting toxicities assessed during first 28-day cycle using NCI CTCAE v5.0. Classical 3+3 escalation proceeded from 100mg BID up to 500mg BID. Maximum tolerated dose not reached; RP2D set at 300mg BID based on PK/PD modeling and safety profile.

AE Capture Methods: Investigator-elicited using CTCAE v5.0 at scheduled visits (Days 1, 8, 15, 22 of Cycle 1, then Day 1 of subsequent cycles). No formal patient-reported outcome tools in dose-escalation phase - potential for subjective symptom under-reporting.

Biomarker Analysis: PIK3CA/AKT/PTEN mutations: ORR 61.5% vs 53.3% wild-type. No strong biomarker effect identified. ESR1 mutation status collected but not selection criterion.

Phase 3 FourLight-3 Trial Design (NCT06760637)

Control Arm Justification: Physician's choice of approved CDK4/6i (palbociclib, ribociclib, or abemaciclib) + letrozole. FDA guidance indicated active comparator necessary given three approved agents with OS benefit. No placebo arm ethical given established SoC.

Sample Size & Power: n=500 powered for non-inferiority (NI margin preserves 50% of CDK4/6i effect) with potential superiority testing if NI met. Assumes HR 1.0 for NI, 0.75 for superiority, 80% power, one-sided α=0.025.

Endpoint Strategy: Primary: PFS by BICR (bias mitigation in open-label). Key secondary: OS (α-controlled), ORR, DoR, time to chemotherapy, safety/tolerability, PROs (EORTC QLQ-C30/BR23).

PTRS Estimate: Technical success ~60% based on Phase 1/2 efficacy. Regulatory success ~40% given comparator heterogeneity and need for clear advantage. Overall PTRS: 24%.

Phase 1 ARV-471-mBC-101 Analysis (NCT04072952)

Design: Open-label, single-arm dose escalation (3+3) with expansion. Parts A (monotherapy), B (expansion), C (+ palbociclib combo). No blinding or concurrent control.

DLT Assessment: Cycle 1 (28 days) window. Escalation from 30mg to 700mg QD. No DLTs observed, MTD not reached. RP2D: 200mg QD based on PK/PD, QTc modeling (predicted ΔQTcF ~6.8ms at 200mg vs ~14.7ms at 500mg).

Safety Monitoring: CTCAE v5.0 investigator-assessed. TEAEs in 82% (most Grade 1-2). Common: nausea (29%), fatigue (20%), hot flush (18%). Grade ≥3 in 20%, mostly lab abnormalities.

PK/Drug Interactions: Vepdegestrant increased palbociclib exposure (requires dose adjustment). P-gp substrate interactions: doubled dabigatran exposure, 11% rosuvastatin increase.

Efficacy Signals: CBR 40% (19/47 evaluable), 3 PRs in 38 with measurable disease. Median duration on treatment 6 months. Two patients ongoing >18 months.

Phase 3 VERITAC-2 Positive Results

Trial Design: Global randomized (1:1), open-label with BICR. 26 countries, n=624. Stratified by ESR1 mutation (43% ESR1-mut), prior CDK4/6i duration, presence of visceral metastases.

Co-Primary Endpoints Met: PFS in ESR1-mut: 5.0 vs 2.1mo (HR 0.57, p<0.001). ITT population: 3.7 vs 3.6mo (HR 0.83, p=0.07) - not significant but directionally favorable.

Secondary Endpoints: Time to first chemotherapy prolonged. OS immature but trending favorable (HR 0.78). Quality of life maintained.

Safety Profile: Grade ≥3 AEs: 16% vs 19% fulvestrant. Most common: nausea (35% all grade, 2% G3), fatigue (22% all, 1% G3). No new safety signals.

Regulatory Path: NDA filing planned Q1 2025 for ESR1-mutant indication. Breakthrough Therapy Designation granted. Priority Review expected.

FDA Requirements & Guidance

Control Arm Mandates

Quote: "The agency tells him what they expect the control arm to be, which has never previously been studied in the context of their drug"

Vepdegestrant: Fulvestrant acceptable as established SoC in 2L setting

Disitamab: Would require T-DXd or T-DM1 for US trial, not physician choice chemo

Lu-NeoB: Will need randomized control for Phase 2/3 - likely ribociclib + fulvestrant alone

Endpoint Requirements

Primary for Registration:

  • PFS by BICR (open-label bias mitigation)
  • OS for full approval (accelerated possible with ORR)
  • Co-primary in biomarker-selected + all-comers (ESR1 precedent)

Sample Size Determination: "They basically put a finger in the wind to come up with a sample size"

Line of Therapy Reality

Quote: "In the United States... approval is usually second line or third line, because we still don't know about that standard of care squishiness"

Even if Phase 3 conducted in 1L, FDA often restricts label to 2L/3L initially due to:

  • Established SoC with OS benefit in 1L (CDK4/6i + ET)
  • Need for longer follow-up to demonstrate OS
  • Subgroup analyses required (visceral, bone-only, etc.)

EMA & Global Considerations

EMA Specific Requirements

Quality of Life Emphasis: PROs required as key secondary, often co-primary for label claims

Subgroup Consistency: Demands forest plots showing benefit across EU countries

Companion Diagnostics: CE-IVD marking required for any biomarker selection (ESR1, GRPR)

NMPA (China) Requirements

Quote: "In clinicaltrials.gov you will see the prior work... you have to disclose that information in your protocol and your investigator brochure"

Bridging Studies:

  • PK study in Chinese patients (n=9-20)
  • Safety run-in often required
  • Can accept global trial with >15% Chinese enrollment
  • Separate China manufacturing often needed

PMDA (Japan) Requirements

Unique Aspects:

  • Step-down dosing often required (start 20% lower)
  • Ichikawa review process adds 6-12 months
  • Ethnic PK always required regardless of global data

"Standard of Care Squishiness" - What It Means

Definition from transcript: "We need to figure out what that standard of care squishiness is"

Refers to scenarios where SoC is poorly defined, heterogeneous, or lacking strong evidence:

  • 2L Post-CDK4/6i: No clear standard - fulvestrant alone vs + everolimus vs chemo all used
  • ESR1-mutant: Elacestrant approved but uptake slow, fulvestrant still common
  • Brain mets: Excluded from most trials, no standard approach
  • Post-ADC progression: Emerging space with no established sequence

Endpoint Requirements

Primary for Registration:

  • PFS by BICR (open-label bias mitigation)
  • OS for full approval (accelerated possible with ORR)
  • Co-primary in biomarker-selected + all-comers (ESR1 precedent)

Sample Size Determination: "They basically put a finger in the wind to come up with a sample size"

Line of Therapy Reality

Quote: "In the United States... approval is usually second line or third line, because we still don't know about that standard of care squishiness"

Even if Phase 3 conducted in 1L, FDA often restricts label to 2L/3L initially due to:

  • Established SoC with OS benefit in 1L (CDK4/6i + ET)
  • Need for longer follow-up to demonstrate OS
  • Subgroup analyses required (visceral, bone-only, etc.)

EMA & Global Considerations

EMA Specific Requirements

Quality of Life Emphasis: PROs required as key secondary, often co-primary for label claims

Subgroup Consistency: Demands forest plots showing benefit across EU countries

Companion Diagnostics: CE-IVD marking required for any biomarker selection (ESR1, GRPR)

NMPA (China) Requirements

Quote: "In clinicaltrials.gov you will see the prior work... you have to disclose that information in your protocol and your investigator brochure"

Bridging Studies:

  • PK study in Chinese patients (n=9-20)
  • Safety run-in often required
  • Can accept global trial with >15% Chinese enrollment
  • Separate China manufacturing often needed

PMDA (Japan) Requirements

Unique Aspects:

  • Step-down dosing often required (start 20% lower)
  • Ichikawa review process adds 6-12 months
  • Ethnic PK always required regardless of global data

"Standard of Care Squishiness" - What It Means

Definition from transcript: "We need to figure out what that standard of care squishiness is"

Refers to scenarios where SoC is poorly defined, heterogeneous, or lacking strong evidence:

  • 2L Post-CDK4/6i: No clear standard - fulvestrant alone vs + everolimus vs chemo all used
  • ESR1-mutant: Elacestrant approved but uptake slow, fulvestrant still common
  • Brain mets: Excluded from most trials, no standard approach
  • Post-ADC progression: Emerging space with no established sequence
