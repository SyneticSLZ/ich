Strategic Importance
Statins remain the foundational therapy that all new drugs must justify adding to, not replacing. Their low cost and proven outcomes create a high bar for cost-effectiveness. Inclisiran and other PCSK9 inhibitors are positioned as add-ons for patients not at goal on maximally tolerated statins, making statins more ally than adversary in the treatment paradigm.
⚙️
Mechanism of Action
Statins work by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase, the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis. This leads to:
  • Reduced intracellular cholesterol in hepatocytes
  • Upregulation of LDL receptors via SREBP-2 activation
  • Increased clearance of LDL from circulation
  • Compensatory increase in PCSK9 production (limiting factor)
  • Pleiotropic effects: anti-inflammatory, plaque stabilization

Competitive Dynamic
Ezetimibe acts as another gatekeeper before expensive therapies. Many patients achieve goals with statin + ezetimibe, never needing PCSK9 inhibitors. Its cheap generic status and benign safety profile make it an automatic second step, potentially limiting inclisiran's addressable market to those still above goal after both statin and ezetimibe.
⚙️
Mechanism of Action
Ezetimibe selectively inhibits NPC1L1 (Niemann-Pick C1-Like 1) transporter at the intestinal brush border, leading to:
  • Reduced absorption of dietary and biliary cholesterol
  • Decreased cholesterol delivery to liver
  • Compensatory upregulation of hepatic LDL receptors
  • Enhanced clearance of LDL from circulation
  • Complementary mechanism to statins

Market Impact on Inclisiran
With proven CV outcomes, bempedoic acid can now market itself as preventing heart attacks - something inclisiran cannot yet claim. Payers may require trying bempedoic acid (±ezetimibe) before approving expensive injectables, creating another step therapy barrier. The 35% LDL reduction with bempedoic+ezetimibe may satisfy many patients, shrinking inclisiran's addressable market.
⚙️
Mechanism of Action
Bempedoic acid inhibits ATP citrate lyase (ACL), an enzyme upstream of HMG-CoA reductase in cholesterol synthesis. Key features:
  • Prodrug activated by ACSVL1 enzyme (liver-specific)
  • Not activated in muscle tissue (avoiding myopathy)
  • Reduces hepatic cholesterol synthesis
  • Upregulates LDL receptors
  • Complementary to statin mechanism
  • Can be used with or without statins

Competitive Threat to Inclisiran
An all-oral triple therapy (statin + obicetrapib + ezetimibe) could rival injectable PCSK9 inhibitor efficacy. With ~50% LDL reduction from obicetrapib+ezetimibe combo, many patients might achieve goals without injections. If approved before oral PCSK9 inhibitors, could gain foothold as the preferred oral intensification option.
⚙️
Mechanism of Action
Obicetrapib inhibits CETP (Cholesteryl Ester Transfer Protein), leading to:
  • Reduced transfer of cholesteryl esters from HDL to VLDL/LDL
  • Enhanced reverse cholesterol transport
  • Indirect upregulation of LDL receptors
  • Substantial HDL-C increase (>100%)
  • Reduction in VLDL remnants
  • No off-target aldosterone effects (unlike torcetrapib)

Relevance to Inclisiran
Evinacumab doesn't compete with inclisiran - it addresses cases where PCSK9 inhibition fails (null LDL receptors). Shows that different mechanisms (ANGPTL3) can lower LDL independently of LDLR, providing options for genetic conditions requiring orthogonal approaches. More complementary than competitive in the treatment landscape.
⚙️
Mechanism of Action
Evinacumab blocks ANGPTL3 (Angiopoietin-like 3), a protein that inhibits lipoprotein and endothelial lipases:
  • LDL receptor-independent pathway
  • Enhanced clearance of VLDL remnants
  • Reduced conversion of VLDL to LDL
  • Dramatically lowers triglycerides
  • Works even with null LDL receptors
  • Complementary to PCSK9 inhibition
