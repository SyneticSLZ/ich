📋 Detailed Information

🎯 Game-Changer: First oral PCSK9 inhibitor - No injections required! Daily tablet provides similar efficacy to injectable monoclonal antibodies with the convenience of oral administration.

Mechanism of Action

Enlicitide decanoate is a macrocyclic peptide (MW ~1 kDa) that acts as an oral PCSK9 inhibitor. Key mechanisms:

  • Binds with high affinity to circulating PCSK9 in plasma
  • Prevents PCSK9 interaction with LDL receptors on hepatocytes
  • Inhibits PCSK9-mediated degradation of LDL-R
  • Increases LDL-R recycling and expression on liver surface
  • Achieves >90% suppression of free PCSK9 (comparable to mAbs)
  • Results in 60-65% LDL-C reduction
  • Survives GI degradation through cyclization and structural modifications

💊 Competitive Advantages vs Injectable PCSK9 Inhibitors

✅ Advantages:
  • Oral administration - no needles
  • At-home convenience
  • No injection site reactions
  • Potentially lower cost
  • Broader prescriber base (GP vs specialist)
⚠️ Considerations:
  • Daily dosing vs biweekly/monthly injections
  • Adherence challenges (~50% real-world)
  • More frequent than inclisiran (2x/year)
  • GI tolerability in some patients

Neurocognitive Safety

No signals in trials with ~30% achieving very low LDL (<25 mg/dL). Confusion/amnesia <1%. Consistent with PCSK9 mAb class data showing no neurocognitive harm even at LDL <15 mg/dL long-term. Oral route unlikely to differ from injectable safety profile.

Unique Safety Considerations

  • No injection site reactions (oral administration)
  • GI tolerability generally good (not dose-related)
  • No CYP metabolism - acts extracellularly
  • Low immunogenicity (<1% anti-drug antibodies)
  • No liver enzyme or glucose elevations
