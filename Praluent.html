Mechanism of Action

PCSK9 Inhibition via Monoclonal Antibody
Praluent is a fully human monoclonal antibody with high affinity binding to circulating PCSK9, preventing its interaction with LDL receptors on hepatocytes. This mechanism leads to:
  • Prevention of PCSK9-mediated LDL-R degradation
  • Increased LDL-R density on liver cell surface
  • Enhanced uptake and catabolism of LDL-C
  • Substantial serum LDL-C reductions (50-60%)
  • Rapid onset within days, peak effect by 4-8 weeks
Key Differentiators: Acts extracellularly in plasma with rapid onset compared to inclisiran's delayed gene-silencing mechanism. Equivalent to evolocumab in PCSK9 inhibition potency but offers lower starting dose for titration flexibility.

ODYSSEY OUTCOMES - Detailed Analysis

Primary & Secondary Endpoints
Primary Composite MACE:
  • CV death, nonfatal MI/stroke, UA hospitalization
  • HR 0.85 (95% CI: 0.78-0.93)
  • 15% relative risk reduction, p<0.001
All-Cause Mortality (Key Finding):
  • HR 0.85 (95% CI: 0.73-0.98)
  • 15% reduction (3.5% vs 4.1%)
  • Nominal p=0.026
Unique Finding: ODYSSEY OUTCOMES showed nominal mortality benefit not seen with evolocumab (FOURIER). Greatest benefit in patients with baseline LDL â¥100 mg/dL: MACE 24% RRR, death 29% RRR. Median follow-up 2.8 years.

Neurocognitive & Metabolic Safety

Long-Term Safety Data
Neurocognitive Effects:
  • Neurocognitive AEs: 1.2% vs 0.7% placebo
  • No association with LDL <25 mg/dL
  • 2025 meta-analyses confirm no link
  • Rare reports non-causal
Metabolic Effects:
  • New-onset diabetes: HR 1.18 (slight increase)
  • Similar to statin effect
  • Ophthalmologic AEs: 1.3% (same as placebo)
  • No cataract increase
Real-World Evidence: 2025 data from Japan shows low AE rates consistent with trials. Benign profile in T1D patients. Similar to inclisiran but with more extensive long-term data.

Clinical Positioning

When to Choose Each Agent
Consider Praluent When:
  • Lower starting dose desired for tolerability
  • Titration flexibility needed
  • Q4W dosing preferred (300 mg option)
  • Younger pediatric patients (8+ vs 10+)
  • Very high-risk patients (mortality benefit)
Consider Repatha When:
  • Fixed dosing simplicity preferred
  • Lower injection site reaction risk important
  • Extensive long-term safety data desired
  • Monthly single-dose option needed
  • Lower immunogenicity concerns
Clinical Equivalence: Both agents are considered therapeutically equivalent for LDL-C reduction and CV risk reduction. Choice often based on formulary coverage, patient preference for dosing regimen, and individual tolerability. The mortality signal with Praluent may influence choice in highest-risk patients.
