PCSK9 Inhibition via Monoclonal Antibody

Praluent is a fully human monoclonal antibody with high affinity binding to circulating PCSK9, preventing its interaction with LDL receptors on hepatocytes.

  • Mechanism: Prevention of PCSK9-mediated LDL-R degradation
  • Effect: Increased LDL-R density on liver cell surface
  • Result: Enhanced uptake and catabolism of LDL-C
  • Reduction: Substantial serum LDL-C reductions (50-60%)
  • Onset: Rapid onset within days, peak effect by 4-8 weeks

Clinical Equivalence: Both agents are considered therapeutically equivalent for LDL-C reduction and CV risk reduction. Choice often based on formulary coverage, patient preference for dosing regimen, and individual tolerability. The mortality signal with Praluent may influence choice in highest-risk patients.

Legacy Position

Praluent pioneered PCSK9 inhibition as first FDA-approved agent. Mortality signal in ODYSSEY OUTCOMES remains unique differentiator. Focus shifting to Lp(a) inhibitors and next-generation lipid therapies.

Sales Performance Gap: Repatha outsells Praluent ~3:1 despite similar efficacy due to Amgen's stronger cardiovascular portfolio presence and better payer relationships. 2019 partnership restructure impacted Praluent momentum.
